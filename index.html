Coffee Type Guide

Overview

The history of coffee goes at least as far back as the 10th centaury, with a number of reports and legends surrounding its first use. The native (undomesticated) origin of coffee is thought to have been Ethiopia. The earliest substantiated evidence of knowledge of the coffee tree is from the 15th centaury, in the Sufi monasteries of Yemen. By the 16th centaury, it had reached the rest of the Middle East, Persia, Turkey, Horn of Africa, and northern Africa. Coffee then spread to the Balkans, Italy and to the rest of Europe, to Indonesia and and then to America.
